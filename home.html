Welcome to the Wildlife Conservation Society!

By Discovering how to Save Nature we can Inspire Everyone to Work with us to Protect Wildlife in the Last Wild Places on Earth.

Founded as The New York Zoological Society on April 26, 1895, The Wildlife Conservation Society is a 501(c)(3) Non-Profit Organization founded by Madison Grant, Andrew H. Green, and Henry F. Osborn. The organization was originally created with the goals to: Open up a zoological park, Promote the study of zoology, and to Preserve wildlife. Today, our society strives to protect and conserve natural wildlife and ecosystems across the globe.

Our goal is to conserve the world's largest wild places in 14 priority regions, habitat for around 50% of the world’s biodiversity and a wide range of charismatic megafauna.

The Wildlife Conservation Society has continued to make global strides towards long-term conservation across The Americas, Africa, Asia, and Oceania. Over the past 100 years WCS has esablished deep partnerships with communities across 14 different regions, aiming to assist in the protection and preservation of all types of animals and wildlife.


Over the past 120 years, WCF have assisted numberous communities and governments to create over 268 National Parks worldwide.



We oversee five wildlife parks/aquariums in New York City including:

The Bronx Zoo, Central Park Zoo, New York Aquarium, Prospect Park Zoo, and The Queens Zoo.
